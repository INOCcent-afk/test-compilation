    Lighting solutions for the healthcare environment

    Complex healthcare environments demand carefully planned and thoughtful lighting solutions. Lighting must be welcoming, calming and healing, whilst aiding staff productivity and wellbeing.

    Coloured LED solutions

    The ever increasing understanding of the effects of light on people within the healthcare environment, coupled with the technological advances within surgical suites, has resulted in an increased demand for a range of coloured LED options.

    Clinical services lorem ipsum

    Colour rendition is crucial in theatres and recovery areas. Ra90+ LEDs are recommended to be used throughout recovery areas, scrub zones, utility rooms and operating theatres

    Presentation areas

    Illuminance must allow hospital staff to conduct the final preparation stages, but at the same time remain calming for the patient.

    Recovery areas

    These areas with selective switching can bring individual control to suit the individuals needs, whilst recovering in multi-bed environments.

    Scrub areas

    Sufficient illuminance must be provided to ensure all staff are able to follow and complete washing protocols.

    Operating Theatres

    In theatres, dimmable solutions are required to allow the correct balance between background and task lighting.

    Our complete cleanroom ranges come with Antimicrobial powder coatings that protect against microbial growth, as standard. By incorporating silver ion antimicrobial technology, our cleanroom luminaires will now be even easier to keep hygienically clean.

    Wards & patients rooms lorem

    In modern hospital wards, ambience can have a direct effect on patient recovery times. The aim is to create a comfortable, relaxing environment giving control of the immediate surroundings to the patient. Glare must also be minimised to make the recumbent patient as comfortable as possible.

    General lighting

    Illuminance should be sufficient to enable safe movement around patient areas. Ambient lighting should provide a relaxing lower level of illumination.

    General care

    Lighting should be adaptable to allow medical practices to be carried out at the bed without intruding on the patients comfort.

    Nightlight

    Lighting should provide sufficient illuminance to allow staff and patients to safely move around. Amber light at night helps to avoid disturbing sleeping patients.

    Observation

    Dimmed observation settings for lighting provides enough background illumination for staff to undertake continuous observation overnight.

    Project

    Interior and exterior luminaires

    Location

    Oxford Falls, NSW, Australia

    Space

    Block K - Senior School Independent Learning & Resources Centre

    Timeline

    May 2019 - January 2020

    21st-century learning spaces in a contemporary school environment

    Oxford Falls Grammar School is a co-educational private school for children from Kindergarten through to Year 12. Thirty years in existence, the school currently educates 1,100 students. Eagle Lighting supplied luminaires for classrooms, study areas, corridors, and breakout space, along with exterior lighting.

    Block K (Senior School Independent Learning and Resources Centre) is an expansion to Oxford Falls Grammar School as part of its 2020 Strategic Plan.

    Project ipsum dolor sit

    The new Block K provides the school with eight additional General Purpose Learning Areas over two stories, senior private study area, private study room, and amenities along with new undercover drop off and pick-up zone.

    The project was an opportunity to set a new standard at Oxford Falls Grammar School in terms of the size of rooms, their flexibility and the standard of fixtures and fittings used. These are 21st-century learning spaces in a contemporary school environment.

    Each level of classrooms comprises of 4 individual spaces, with over-sized concourses that allow for student movement while creating additional learning space for classes to utilise. Each floor offers maximum flexibility whereby the learning spaces can open up into one large open area for year group meetings and presentations.

    Meet us at Euroshop 2021 in Düsseldorf

    Category / Category

    Date

    March 16, 2021 - 3 days

    Location

    Oxford Falls, NSW, Australia

    Area

    Hall 9 - Stand E73

    Links

    Visit website

    On 16-18 March, we will exhibit at Euroshop in Dusseldorf. 120,000 m2 with 115,000 attendees and 2,500 exhibitors from 60 countries makes the trade fair one of the largest in the world.

    Visit Fagerhult’s stand and experience light. Get to know the latest within lighting, see new products such as the stylish spotlight family and innovative lighting controls. Get inspired with just how lighting can help to create a total retail experience in a shop.

    Monday - Meet Malin Isberg from the company Lexter whom create shop experiences by using sound and scent design.

    Tuesday - Meet Henrik Clausen from Fagerhult Lighting Academy and talk about the importance of light and how light affect us as humans.

    Wednesday - Meet Monica Weinitz the Managing director at Fagerhult Retail AB and talk about our global offering.

    Thursday - Meet Peter Robertson and Simon Wang, the product manager and design engineer manager behind the new Touch spotlight series.

    Get Inspired

    At our stand you can try out different light settings and see what difference light makes. In our Lookbook for lighting you can find the right look for your brand. Get inspired at: fagerhult.com/lookbook.

    Competition

    Each day there is a possibility to win a Audio Pro T3 at our stand. We invite you to enter. The winner will be announced on Fagerhult's Facebook page at the end of each day.
